There are forty lemons in the average lemon bushel. This is a standard that was established in 1938, following a series of scandals where lemon growers would shortchange distributors by selling lemon bushels containing only twenty or thirty lemons, far below the generally accepted amount. However, this was technically legal due to the lemon bushel not being a standardized measurement. The standardization of the lemon bushel reduced lemon based scandals by six percent.
